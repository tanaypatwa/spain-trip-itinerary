Madrid · the football leg
21
Sunday · Arrival
Land in Madrid
Spain plays at 6 PM — the whole day builds to it.
AM

Land, drop bags, breathe

Get to base: TOC Hostel, Plaza de Celenque — right between Puerta del Sol and Gran Vía, dead center and a 10-min walk to the Malasaña/Chueca bars. Freshen up, don't over-plan; you're off an overnight flight (Spain is 3.5h behind Bombay, so jet lag is mild).

From TOC: fan zone at Plaza de Colón ≈ 20-min walk · Bernabéu ≈ 25 min by metro · Atocha (València train) ≈ one Cercanías stop / 10 min.

1 PM
Easy first meal

Tapas + a slow wander

Lunch at Mercado de San Miguel, then a loop through Puerta del Sol, Plaza Mayor and the Royal Palace exterior. Keep it light — save the legs.

4:30 PM
★ Match day

Spain vs Saudi Arabia — fan zone

Kickoff is 6 PM Madrid time. Head to the official giant screen at Plaza de Colón — free, enormous crowd, full La Roja chaos. Get there ~90 min early to claim space.

Group backup if the square's a crush: any sports bar in Malasaña will have it on — easier to keep 8+ people together.

After the final whistle ◗ ~8 PM

First night out

Ride the win (hopefully) into a tapas-crawl dinner, then ease in: rooftop drinks at Círculo de Bellas Artes → bars in Malasaña. If anyone's still standing, Teatro Kapital (7 floors, Madrid institution) is the move. Don't burn out on night one.

22
Monday · Madrid
Bernabéu + the city
Your one full Madrid day. Make it count.
10 AM
★ Must-do

Santiago Bernabéu tour

The newly renovated stadium — museum, pitch-side, panoramic views. Open Mon–Sat 9–19, self-guided. Book your slot online in advance so you're not stuck in the queue.

2 PM

Retiro + Gran Vía

Row a boat in El Retiro, then walk Gran Vía for the big-city buzz and shopping. Pick one museum if you're into it (Prado / Reina Sofía) — optional.

9 PM
Dinner

Late Spanish dinner

Eat properly — Spaniards dine at 9–10. Then the night's just starting.

Big Madrid night ◗ this is the one

Go all in

Pre-game in Malasaña / Chueca bars, then a proper club: Teatro Kapital or Teatro Barceló. Spanish clubs don't fill till ~1–2 AM and run till dawn — pace accordingly, you've got a train tomorrow.

València · fire on the beach
23
Tuesday · Madrid → València
Train south + Noche de San Juan
The festival you came for happens tonight.
~11 AM
Travel

High-speed train, Atocha → València

1h40–1h55, ~20+ departures a day. Take a late-morning / early-afternoon train so you're settled before the beach roads close (~4–5 PM tonight). Book ahead on Renfe / iryo / Ouigo — fares drop to €9–10 early.

2 PM

Check in near the beach

Base yourself near Malvarrosa / Patacona (or El Carmen old town with easy tram to the sand). Stock up on food + drinks for tonight — it's BYO on the beach.

★ Noche de San Juan ◗ evening → ~4 AM

Bonfires on Malvarrosa

All of València pours onto the beach: bonfires, music, food, midnight jump into the sea to "welcome summer." Head down early evening to grab a spot. The party runs till around 4 AM — this is the heart of your València stop.

24
Wednesday · València
Recover + the futuristic city
Slow start earned. Then the good stuff.
Noon
★ Eat where it was invented

Real paella lunch

València is the birthplace of paella — have the authentic thing (try near the beach or El Palmar). Lazy, long, deserved.

4 PM
★ Photo gold

City of Arts & Sciences

Spaceship-like white architecture, reflecting pools — the most photogenic spot in town. Bike the Turia Gardens (a riverbed-turned-park) to get there, then wander the old town: Cathedral, Mercado Central.

Lighter night ◗ recharge

Bars in El Carmen

Easy night out in the Barrio del Carmen — buzzy, young, low-key. You've got an island and three more nights ahead; bank some energy.

Ibiza · the island
25
Thursday · València → Ibiza
Fly to the island
Sunsets and superclubs from here on.
AM
Travel

València → Ibiza

Quickest is a direct flight (~45 min) on Vueling/Iberia; a fast ferry (Baleària, ~2–3h) is the scenic alt. Book early — summer island seats vanish.

PM

Check in: Playa d'en Bossa or San Antonio

Playa d'en Bossa = next to Ushuaïa & Hï (clubs at your doorstep). San Antonio = the legendary sunset strip + pool parties. Pick your vibe.

★ Island intro ◗ golden hour

Sunset at Café del Mar

Ease in with the famous San Antonio sunset (Café del Mar / Café Mambo), then test the waters at one club. Pre-book tickets / guest list — door prices on the island are brutal otherwise.

26
Friday · Ibiza
Full island day
Pool party → sunset → superclub.
Day
★ The Ibiza thing

Pool / boat party

O Beach or Ibiza Rocks for the daytime party, or a boat day around the coast. This is peak-Ibiza energy.

Evening

Sunset strip, round two

Back to San Antonio for the sky show before the night kicks off.

Superclub night ◗ the big one

Pick your temple

Ushuaïa (open-air), Hï Ibiza, Pacha, Amnesia or DC10 — check the 2026 line-ups and chase the DJ you want. Tables/GL for a big group pay off.

⚽ Fun footnote: Spain's 3rd match (vs Uruguay) kicks off ~2 AM Spain time tonight — you'll likely be mid-club when it's on the screens.

Barcelona · the finale
27
Saturday · Ibiza → Barcelona
Land + Saturday night out
You specifically wanted BCN nightlife on a Saturday. Here it is.
Midday
Travel

Ibiza → Barcelona

Flight ~55 min, very frequent. Take a morning/midday one so you land with the afternoon to spare and the night intact. Base in El Born / Gothic (nightlife + walkable) or near Barceloneta beach.

Evening
Fuel up

Tapas + vermut in El Born

Settle in, eat well, then prep for the main event.

★ Saturday night ◗ the headline

Barcelona, full volume

Beachfront mega-clubs at Port Olímpic — Opium, Pacha Barcelona, Shôko — or the legendary multi-room Razzmatazz. Saturday is the night the city peaks. Book a table / guest list ahead for the group.

28
Sunday · Barcelona
Recover + Gaudí icons
Tourist day. Also a chance to nurse last night.
Late AM
★ Must-do

Sagrada Família + Park Güell

Gaudí's masterpieces. Both need timed tickets booked in advance — do it now, Sundays sell out. Add the Gothic Quarter and a stroll down La Rambla.

Afternoon

Barceloneta beach

Sun, swim, chiringuito drinks. Recovery mode with a sea view.

Chill night ◗ skydiving tomorrow

Keep it mellow

Dinner + a few drinks, early-ish night. You want clear heads (and stomachs) before jumping out of a plane.

29
Monday · Costa Brava
Skydive day
Block the whole day — travel + weather buffer.
Full day
★ Bucket-list

Skydive Costa Brava, Empuriabrava

Sector Aeroclub s/n, 17487 Empuriabrava (Girona) — one of the world's top dropzones, coastal views the whole way down. It's ~1.5–2h north of Barcelona, so do it as a full-day trip: organized transfer or train + taxi, plus a buffer for weather holds.

Weather plan: if Monday's a wash, swap with Sunday 28 — keep both days loosely flexible. Book the jump slot in advance either way.

Evening
Farewell

Last proper dinner

Back in BCN — toast the trip. Adrenaline + good food, light on the rest.

30
Tuesday · Departure
Last hours + fly home
Flight out 22:30 — you have the whole day.
AM

Brunch + last looks

Lazy brunch, then either the Bunkers del Carmel viewpoint for the best skyline in the city, or Passeig de Gràcia for last-minute shopping. Check bags into a luggage store so you're hands-free.

7:30 PM
Wheels up

Head to BCN airport

Be airside by ~7:30–8 PM for the 10:30 PM long-haul home. Adéu, España.
